Numerical Methods Assignment #11

Integrating with the Trapezoid Method

This assignment will be done only in Python 3 with a Jupyter-Notebook. You will use the template provided here.

You will create an integration toolbox with a class; it must contain a member method that integrates an equation object (passed as a parameter) using the Trapezoid Numerical Method. You must preserve code encapsulation by instantiating the integration toolbox only once for the entire assignment and by avoiding outside alteration of the toolbox's member data (either directly or through getter/setter functions).

The Scenario...

During a laboratory experiment, a meter records power levels and their associated times, storing them in a text file. Part-way into the experiment, the meter fails. The experiment engineer has determined that the power levels during the downtime are best estimated by the equation given hereafter. After the meter resumes monitoring the experiment, it records the power levels in a step-data format and writes them to a CSV file.

To obtain the total energy involved in the experiment, you must integrate all of the data. Although you must print out the integrals of the individual pieces (for grading purposes), you must also integrate the entire experiment with only one call to your trapezoid method, your time bounds being the beginning and end of the entire experiment. (In other words, you cannot simply add the three integrated sections to obtain the total.) In doing this, your equation object must contain supporting methods to its main equation method that handle the three separate pieces of the experiment. When printing the individual pieces, change the time bounds in your trapezoid method call. Your integration step size is 15 minutes and must be specified through a variable passed as a parameter to your integration method.

In summary, the power levels for the three experiment sections are given as follows:

  • For 0 ≤ t ≤ 72 hours, power levels are given in the linear data text file. (You must use the text file reader.)
  • For 72 hours < t < 96 hours, the equation is... $\frac{1}{5} sin\left(\frac{\pi}{12}\left(t+15\right)\right)+\frac{3}{5}$
  • For 96 hours ≤ t ≤ 9 days, use the CSV step data. (You must use the CSV module's reader.) Note that time zero in the step data file is 96 hours absolute time. For each $\Delta t$ in the step data, the corresponding power level was the average power level during that time period.
