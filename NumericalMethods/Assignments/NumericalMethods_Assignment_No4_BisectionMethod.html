Using the Bisection Method

You might consider plotting the the first two equations with matplotlib to see where you should set the bracketing values for the Bisection Method. The result's accuracy must be to at least four decimal places. The assigned equations and associated roots to solve for are as follows:

  1. Find both roots of the quadratic equation $$x^2=\frac{4}{3}x + \frac{5}{9}$$
  2. Find the first positive root of $cos(x)$
  3. Find the friction factor $f$ in the Colebrook-White equation $$\frac{1}{\sqrt{f}}=-2 log\left(\frac{\varepsilon}{3.7D_h}+\frac{2.51}{Re\sqrt{f}}\right),$$ with the following values:
    • $\varepsilon$ = 1.5e-4
    • $D_h$ = 0.315
    • $Re$ = 6000

Solving symbolically with SymPy

For this portion of the assignment, use the appropriate solver in the SymPy library (hint: it's not "solve") to symbolically find $x$ in the following equations:

  1. Equation # 1 (the quadratic equation) listed for the Bisection Method portion above
  2. The quadratic equation $$0=a x^2 + b x + c.$$ Note that you should obtain both parts of the quadratic formula for your answer.
