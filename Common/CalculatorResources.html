Calculator Resources

A calculator with "textbook" or Reverse Polish Notation (RPN) entry modes is strongly recommended. An algebraic entry calculator that does not display the full entry line before computation is very much discouraged.

HP

  • hpcalc.org - Community repository of HP calculator software & help

HP Graphing Calculators

  • HP 48g Series
    • Legendary
    • Has RPN (main entry mode), textbook, & algebraic entry modes
    • Long battery life (uses 3 AAA's)
  • HP 50g
    • Faster than the 48g
    • Has RPN, textbook (really good), & algebraic entry modes
    • Uses 4 AAA's (rechargeable batteries are recommended)
    • Can use spreadsheet files with add-in program (see hpcalc.org)
  • User Manuals from HP
    • "HP 50g_users guide_English_E_DCVL5300788.pdf" is the main user's guide.
    • "HP 48gII and 50g Graphing Calculator Advanced User's Reference Manual (V2)" provides more information for programming along with a reference of calculator commands.
    • "HP 50g_user's manual_English_HDPSG49AEM8.pdf" is a quick-start guide.

HP 35s Scientific Calculator

  • Has RPN & algebraic entry modes
  • Programmable
  • The most capable calculator allowed on the FE exam
  • HP 35s User Manual
  • Other resources from HP

Casio

Casio fx-115ESPLUS

  • Has textbook & algebraic entry modes
  • Solar powered (great as a backup calculator for exams)
  • Some solver functionality
  • The cost is under $20
  • Be aware that the FX300ES Plus has less capability than the fx-115ESPlus
  • User's Guide for the fx-115ES PLUS & fx-991ES PLUS C
